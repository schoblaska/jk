# Sun, Jun 27 1937
tags: #journal

## Mulwray Is Dead

[Hollis Mulwray](h1m0) was found in the [Oak Pass Reservoir](o1p0) this morning. Drowned. Police are calling it accidental - "drunk engineer falls in reservoir." Neat, clean, case closed.

Except it isn't. I got close enough to hear the coroner talking. Salt water in his lungs. [Oak Pass is freshwater](s1w0). He didn't drown there. He was killed somewhere else and dumped.

Everything he was investigating - the [water dumping](d1r0), the [discrepancies](ai/w1d0), the midnight trips to the outfalls - he was getting close to proving the drought was manufactured. And now he's dead in the reservoir he was trying to protect.

[Evelyn](e1c0) is a widow. And I'm working a murder.

## Lt. Escobar

[Lou Escobar](l1e0) showed up at the scene. We go back - he knew me when I was on the force in Chinatown. Decent cop, but a political one. He pulled me aside and told me to walk away. "It's not your case, Jake. Don't make it your case."

When a cop who knows you tells you to back off, it means someone above him is leaning. Escobar doesn't scare easy. Whoever killed Mulwray has reach into the department.

But Evelyn's my client, and dead husbands change the math on everything.

## The Nose

Caught snooping around the reservoir after the cops cleared out. Two men were waiting. The big one held me. The small one did the talking - [Claude Mulvihill](c1m0), says he works for the water department. Security.

Then the big one put a knife to my nose and cut it. Not deep enough to take it off. Deep enough to send a message.

"You know what happens to nosy fellows? They lose their noses."

Message received. Message ignored. But I'll remember the face.
