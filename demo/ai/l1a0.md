## The Problem

Noah Cross controls or influences most of the institutions that would normally hold him accountable. This analysis considers each avenue honestly.

## Los Angeles District Attorney
tags: #corruption

- Current DA Buron Fitts has faced his own corruption scandals and is not known for challenging powerful interests
- Cross has donated to Fitts's campaigns and to the campaigns of three of the five county supervisors
- A prosecution would require the DA to go against his own donor base and the city's most powerful citizen
- **Likelihood of action: very low**

## LAPD

- [Lt. Escobar](../l1e0) was pressured to close the [Mulwray](../h1m0) case as accidental
- The police commission includes members with ties to Cross's business interests
- Even a sympathetic officer would face institutional resistance
- The [salt water evidence](../s1w0) would need to come from the coroner, who answers to the same political structure
- **Likelihood of independent investigation: very low**

## Federal Options
tags: #legal

- FBI jurisdiction is limited - fraud and conspiracy charges related to the [land scheme](../l1g0) could theoretically qualify
- However, federal interest in local California land fraud is historically minimal
- The [water diversion](w1d0) might constitute interference with a public utility, but enforcement would require local cooperation
- **Likelihood of federal intervention: low**

## Press Exposure
tags: #media

- The [LA Times](l1t0) has already demonstrated its alignment with development interests
- Smaller papers (the Examiner, the Herald) have less institutional protection against retaliation
- A comprehensive leak to a sympathetic journalist could create public pressure
- Risk: Cross could suppress the story through advertising leverage or legal threats
- **Likelihood of meaningful coverage: moderate, but uncertain**

## Civil Action

- [Evelyn Mulwray](../e1c0) could potentially file civil suits related to the [conspiracy](t1c0) - wrongful death, fraud
- Civil cases have a lower burden of proof than criminal
- However, Cross's legal resources are effectively unlimited, and the discovery process would expose Katherine's parentage publicly
- Evelyn is unlikely to pursue this path for that reason

## Assessment

The honest answer is that there is no reliable mechanism for prosecuting Noah Cross within the current power structure of Los Angeles. He has insulated himself legally through [proxies](a1x0), politically through donations, and institutionally through influence over law enforcement. The best hope is documentation - a complete record of the [conspiracy](t1c0) that outlasts the current political moment and becomes usable when the balance of power shifts. If it ever does.
