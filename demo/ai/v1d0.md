## What Happened
tags: #disaster

On March 12, 1928, the Van der Lip Dam in San Francisquito Canyon failed catastrophically just before midnight. The concrete gravity dam, completed only two years earlier, collapsed and sent a wall of water 140 feet high through the canyon and into the Santa Clara River valley.

## The Toll
tags: #casualties

- 500+ confirmed dead, though exact numbers were never established
- Entire communities wiped out: Castaic Junction, Piru, Fillmore, Santa Paula
- Bodies recovered as far as the Pacific Ocean, 54 miles downstream
- Thousands left homeless; property damage in the tens of millions

## The Cause

Post-collapse investigation found the dam was built on unstable geology - one abutment rested on an ancient landslide, the other on a fault zone. Warning signs (seepage, cracks) were reported and dismissed.

## Mulwray's Connection
tags: #motivation

[Hollis Mulwray](../h1m0) was assistant engineer on the Van der Lip project. He was not the lead decision-maker, but he was close enough to see how institutional pressure overrode engineering judgment. Colleagues say the disaster changed him - made him the cautious, stubborn engineer he is today.

His opposition to the [Alto Vallejo Dam](../a1v0) is rooted here. He sees the same red flags: political pressure to build, inadequate geological survey, money moving faster than science.
