## Summary

A comparison of LADWP's public reporting on reservoir levels with available flow data from intake and distribution records reveals significant discrepancies over the past three months.

## Findings
tags: #data-analysis

**Reported to the public (LADWP monthly bulletins):**
- Reservoir system at 41% capacity (March), 38% (April), 34% (May)
- Narrative: severe drought conditions, conservation mandatory

**Calculated from flow records:**
- Aqueduct intake from Owens Valley: steady, consistent with prior non-drought years
- Distribution to residential/commercial: slightly reduced (conservation compliance)
- Reservoir levels should be approximately 58–62% based on inflow minus outflow

**The gap:** roughly 14,000–18,000 acre-feet of water over three months that entered the system but is not reflected in reservoir levels or legitimate distribution

## Where Is the Water Going?
tags: #diversion

- **Ocean outfalls**: Nighttime discharge observed by [Mulwray](../h1m0) at Point Fermin and Playa del Rey - these are emergency overflow channels, not normally active during drought conditions
- **Unscheduled releases**: Several instances of reservoir gates opened during off-hours, logged as "maintenance" with no corresponding work orders
- **Agricultural runoff channels**: Flow recorded in channels serving the northwest valley, where the [wells have reportedly failed](../o1p0) - contradictory, as failed wells would reduce runoff, not increase it

## Assessment

The data supports the theory that the [drought](../d1r0) is being manufactured or significantly exaggerated through deliberate water diversion. The scale of the discrepancy - thousands of acre-feet - requires someone with operational access to LADWP infrastructure. This is not a rogue employee; it requires coordination at or near the top of the department, or external pressure on department operations.
