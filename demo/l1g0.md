# The Land Grab
date: [Fri, Jun 25 1937](1937-06-25)
tags: #theory, #conspiracy

Working theory: someone is buying up the northwest San Fernando Valley on the cheap while the [engineered drought](d1r0) drives farmers to sell. Once the [Alto Vallejo Dam](a1v0) gets approved and built with public money, water flows to the valley, and the land values explode.

The [property transfer records](ai/p1t0) show dozens of parcels changing hands in the last six months. Different buyers every time - retirement homes, church trusts, elderly individuals. All fronts. The pattern is too coordinated to be coincidence.

This is the shape of it:
- Manufacture the drought → farmers go broke
- Buy the land cheap through proxies
- Push the dam through city council (Mulwray was the obstacle)
- [Discredit Mulwray](ai/l1t0) so he can't oppose it
- Dam gets built, water flows, land values multiply

The question is who. Someone with enough money to buy a valley, enough connections to manipulate the water department, and enough pull to get photos on the [front page of the Times](ai/l1t0).

[Noah Cross](n1c0) keeps coming to mind. But that's still just a feeling.
